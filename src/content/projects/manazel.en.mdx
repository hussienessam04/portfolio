The interesting part of Manazel wasn't the map. Mapbox GL is excellent and
mostly config.

The interesting part was what to *not* show. Most Saudi portals treat a
listing like a classified ad: fourteen photos, six bullet points, a phone
number, call. We watched first-time buyers use these sites and they were
paralyzed by the volume.

We rebuilt the search page so the map is the page. Listings are points.
Hovering a point highlights a neighborhood. Clicking opens a panel — not a
new page — with school catchment, transit walkshed, and a five-year price
history that lets a buyer notice when "the prices went up" was actually
2021 and not last month.

RTL was the second-most-interesting part. Mapbox GL handles right-to-left
labels fine, but the search filters don't, and a slider that controls a
price range reads completely differently in Arabic numerals.
