PlaySync started as a way to keep playing Bomberman with a college roommate who
moved to Berlin. We tried Parsec and found out my apartment's upload speed
should not be classified as broadband.

The rewrite drops video entirely. Each player holds the controller; PlaySync
sends the input state over a WebRTC data channel and renders locally. The
"CRT" effect — scanlines, chromatic aberration, a phosphor afterglow — is
CSS and Canvas filters, not shaders, so it runs on a five-year-old phone.

The trickiest thing wasn't networking, it was the audio. WebAudio's
AudioContext is per-tab and resampling-quirky; I ended up with a small
resampler that runs on the main thread and never produces a click under load.
