Rawi started as a side project for a friend who teaches classical Arabic
literature and could not link to a single verse in any online archive. Every
link ended at "page 47" of a 1983 edition that only two libraries own.

The data model is the simplest part of the whole project. A collection has
poems; a poem has verses; a verse has text, meter, and rhyme. The hard part
was the typography. Classical Arabic poetry sits inside a tight prosodic
grid, and most web fonts treat it as a paragraph of prose. Rawi uses
VocalType's Amiri at display scale and falls back to system UI at body scale,
which costs nothing and looks respectful of the form.

A future version will let readers cross-reference a single verse across the
38 collections. That is a search problem, not a typing problem, and I am
waiting until I have a corpus large enough that the search results are
interesting.
