Khayal is named after the Arabic word for "imagination," and it started because
my niece wanted me to make up a story about a rabbit on a train and I kept
forgetting what the rabbit's name was.

The design constraint is brutal: kids don't read, they listen, and their
attention budget on a tablet is roughly four minutes. Khayal uses the Web
Speech API for the voice and OpenAI for the model, but the model output is
post-processed into a strict JSON schema so the rabbit's name is the rabbit's
name by turn three, not "the rabbit" or "the bunny."

It is also the first project I shipped as a PWA. No app store, no review
queue, no five-year-old yelling "DOWNLOAD THE RABBIT APP" at a Saudi telco
customer service rep.
